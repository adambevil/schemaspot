Most local business websites are invisible to Google.

Not because they don't rank. But because they don't tell Google what they are.

I've been auditing local business websites as part of my work, and the pattern is consistent:

A plumber gets page 1 rankings but gets 60% fewer clicks than a competitor with the same ranking position.

Why? The competitor has Rich Snippets — star ratings, business hours, FAQ answers, service areas — all showing directly in the search results.

The plumber doesn't.

The difference is structured data (schema markup) — code on the website that tells Google exactly what the business is and what it does.

Google's own data shows that pages with schema markup get 30% higher click-through rates. For a local business that already ranks well, that's free traffic they're leaving on the table.

The problem: Writing schema markup requires knowing technical SEO. Most small business owners don't have that expertise, and hiring an SEO agency costs $1,000-5,000 for basic implementation.

So I built a free tool to fix this.

It's called SchemaSpot, and it generates valid JSON-LD schema markup for 10+ local business types (restaurants, medical clinics, contractors, gyms, salons, auto repair, hotels, and more) in under 60 seconds.

No sign-up. No paywall. No technical knowledge required.

It even shows you a preview of how your business will look in Google search results before you deploy anything.

You just paste the code into your site header and validate with Google's Rich Results Test.
